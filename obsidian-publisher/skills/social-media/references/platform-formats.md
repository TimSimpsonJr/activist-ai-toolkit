# Platform Formats Reference

## LinkedIn

### Constraints
- **Character limit:** 3,000 for posts
- **Link preview:** LinkedIn generates a preview card when a URL is included
- **Formatting:** Supports bold, italic, bullet points, and line breaks

### Structure

```
[1-2 sentence hook with the most compelling fact]

[2-3 short paragraphs expanding on the key points. Each paragraph 1-2 sentences. Line breaks between paragraphs are important because LinkedIn collapses whitespace.]

[Link to full post]

[3-5 hashtags at the bottom, not inline]
```

### Tone
Slightly more professional than the blog, but still the voice-dna.md voice. Contractions are fine. Parenthetical asides work. Avoid corporate-speak.

### Hashtags
3-5 relevant hashtags at the bottom. Use a mix of broad and specific:
- Broad: `#privacy`, `#surveillance`, `#4thAmendment`
- Specific: `#FlockSafety`, `#ALPR`, `#SouthCarolina`

### Example Pattern

```
[Surprising stat or fact from the post.]

[1-2 sentences of context. Why this matters.]

[1-2 sentences connecting to the reader. What this means for them.]

[Full post link]

#relevant #hashtags #here
```

## X/Twitter

### Constraints
- **Character limit:** 280 per tweet
- **Links:** URLs count as 23 characters regardless of length
- **Thread format:** Numbered (1/N), each tweet should stand alone

### Single Tweet

Best for: posts with one killer fact.

```
[The single most shareable fact from the post, under 257 characters]

[Link]
```

Keep it tight. One fact, one link. No hashtags unless they're genuinely discoverable (use at most 1-2).

### Thread

Best for: posts with multiple compelling points.

Structure:
1. **Tweet 1 (hook):** The most surprising fact + "A thread." or similar
2. **Tweets 2-4:** One key point per tweet, each able to stand alone
3. **Final tweet:** Link to the full post + brief CTA

Keep threads to 3-6 tweets. Longer threads lose readers.

Each tweet in a thread should:
- Make sense if read in isolation (someone might quote-tweet just one)
- End with a natural break (don't split sentences across tweets)
- Include the thread number (1/5, 2/5, etc.)

### Tone
Punchiest version of the voice. Shortest sentences. Most concrete facts. No wasted words.

### Hashtags
1-2 max, only if genuinely discoverable. Skip them entirely if the tweet is stronger without them.

## Facebook

### Constraints
- **Character limit:** 63,206 (effectively unlimited for our purposes)
- **Link preview:** Facebook generates a preview card from the URL
- **Formatting:** Limited. No bold/italic in regular posts. Line breaks work.

### Structure

```
[2-3 sentence hook. More conversational than LinkedIn. Can include a question or personal reaction.]

[2-4 short paragraphs telling the story. Facebook rewards narrative and emotional connection more than LinkedIn's professional tone.]

[Link to full post]
```

### Tone
Most conversational of the three platforms. Still voice-dna.md voice, but lean into the parenthetical asides, honest reactions, and the more personal register. If the framing is "org," the "we" voice can be warmer here than on LinkedIn.

Facebook audiences engage more with stories and local relevance than with statistics alone. Lead with a story or incident, then support with the numbers.

### Hashtags
Optional. Facebook hashtags have lower discovery value than other platforms. Use 1-3 only if they're relevant to a local community or active conversation. Skip them if the post is stronger without.

## General Principles

### Links
- Every platform post includes a link to the full blog post
- Place the link after the main content, not at the top
- On X, the link can be the last thing in the tweet
- On LinkedIn and Facebook, the link generates a preview card

### Images
If the blog post has a compelling visual (map, chart, screenshot):
- Reference it in the social post if applicable
- Note to the user that an image would strengthen the post
- Don't fabricate or describe images that don't exist

### Timing
If the content is time-sensitive (a bill is moving, a new report dropped):
- Note the date/context in the post
- Suggest to the user when to publish for maximum relevance
- Flag if the content may become stale quickly

### Cross-Platform Coordination
Each platform gets a different angle on the same post:
- **LinkedIn:** Professional/civic angle. "Here's what this means for policy."
- **X/Twitter:** The punchiest single fact. Quick hit.
- **Facebook:** The story/narrative angle. "Here's what happened and why it matters to us."

Don't just shorten the LinkedIn post for Twitter or lengthen the tweet for Facebook. Each platform gets content written specifically for how people read on that platform.
